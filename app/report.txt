Risk Factors:
- 18-month horizon increases assumption drift and market changes
- Demand assumptions not fully validated; utilization risk
- Non-trivial capital outlay relative to upside; payback sensitive to volume
- Implementation risk: installation downtime and ramp-up learning curve
- Ongoing maintenance, consumables, and energy costs may erode ROI

Upside Factors:
- Higher throughput enabling revenue lift and backlog reduction
- Lower unit costs from efficiency gains
- Shorter lead times improving win rate and customer satisfaction
- Quality/consistency improvements reducing rework and scrap
- Potential energy efficiency and tax/depreciation benefits
- Option value: scalable capacity if demand materializes

Scenarios:
- Best Case: Demand meets/exceeds forecast; quick ramp to target throughput; minimal downtime; payback within ~6–9 months and sustained margin lift.
- Base Case: Throughput improves as expected; demand grows modestly; staged rollout confirms assumptions; payback within ~12–15 months, meeting ROI threshold.
- Worst Case: Demand lags; equipment underutilized; longer installation/ramp; higher TCO than planned; limited resale recovery; negative return over horizon.

Mitigations:
- Stage the purchase (pilot line or limited units) with clear KPI gates (utilization, cycle time, yield).
- Use rental/lease or negotiated return/resale clauses to preserve reversibility.
- Pre-validate demand via preorders, contracts, or pricing tests before full rollout.
- Complete TCO analysis covering maintenance, consumables, energy, and operator time.
- Develop an installation and changeover plan to minimize downtime; schedule during low-demand windows.
- Conduct vendor due diligence: warranty, SLAs, spare parts lead times, and upgrade paths.
- Run on-site trials to verify throughput gains and compatibility with existing processes.
- Define exit criteria and a kill-switch if KPIs are missed; tranche funding accordingly.
- Provide operator training and SOPs to accelerate ramp and reduce quality losses.

Missing Information:
- Validated demand forecast and signed customer commitments
- Baseline throughput, current bottlenecks, and end-to-end capacity map
- Full TCO: maintenance, consumables, energy, calibration, and training costs
- Installation timeline, expected downtime, and ramp-to-steady-state curve
- Financing terms vs cash purchase; impact on cash flow
- Resale value estimates, lease options, and return policies
- Warranty coverage, SLAs, and spare parts availability
- Space, utility, and safety/compliance requirements
- Integration/compatibility with existing equipment and software
- Vendor lead time and delivery schedule
- Depreciation method and tax treatment
- Empirical evidence of expected throughput uplift (pilot or vendor trials)

Red Flags:
- Proceeding without demand validation or customer commitments
- All-at-once purchase instead of staged rollout
- Single-vendor lock-in without protections or alternatives
- Underestimating installation downtime and training needs
- Ignoring upstream/downstream constraints that limit throughput gains
- No defined KPI gates, exit criteria, or contingency plan
